Frequently asked

How do I add a city?

Tap the list icon in the top-right of the main forecast page, then tap "Add city" and search by name. Halcyon supports up to three saved cities on the Free and Ad-Free tiers.

Why are my forecasts different from another weather app?

Halcyon uses Apple's WeatherKit service for all forecast data. Other apps may use different providers (Carrot, AccuWeather, Weather.com, Tomorrow.io, etc.), each with its own model and update cadence. None is universally "right." For severe weather, always check your local meteorological service in addition to Halcyon.

Halcyon says "Showing the last saved forecast" — what does that mean?

You're viewing cached data from your last successful refresh, usually because the device is offline or WeatherKit is temporarily unavailable. Pull down on the forecast page to retry, or reconnect to Wi-Fi or cellular and the forecast will refresh automatically.

How do I subscribe to Ad-Free?

Open Halcyon → tap the gear icon (Settings) → tap "Remove ads". The paywall walks you through the purchase. You can also tap any ad slot in the app and select "Remove ads".

How do I cancel Ad-Free?

Subscription cancellation is handled by Apple, not in-app. Open iOS Settings → tap your Apple ID at the top → tap "Subscriptions" → tap "Halcyon Ad-Free" → tap "Cancel Subscription". You'll keep ad-free access until the end of the current billing period.

I subscribed on another device — how do I get Ad-Free here?

On the new device, open Halcyon → Settings → "Remove ads" → tap "Restore Purchases" on the paywall. Make sure you're signed in to the same Apple ID that purchased the subscription. Restoration usually takes a few seconds.

I want a refund.

Refunds are handled by Apple. Visit reportaproblem.apple.com, sign in with your Apple ID, find the Halcyon transaction, and request a refund. Apple reviews each request individually and decides whether to issue one.

Severe weather alert didn't fire — what happened?

Halcyon schedules severe-weather notifications based on the alerts WeatherKit returns. If you didn't receive one, check: (a) Notifications are enabled for Halcyon in iOS Settings → Notifications → Halcyon; (b) "Severe weather alerts" is on in Halcyon → Settings → Notifications; (c) the alert was actually issued by your local weather authority. Always cross-reference Halcyon with your local emergency broadcast system — Halcyon is not a substitute for official severe-weather alerts.

How do I delete my data?

Halcyon stores saved cities and preferences locally on your device only — we do not have your data on a server. Deleting Halcyon from your device removes everything.
